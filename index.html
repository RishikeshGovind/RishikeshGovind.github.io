Financial Analyst

The Data Deluge Dilemma

Every trading desk and quant team today is awash in data—market ticks, alternative signals, economic indicators—yet raw volume alone doesn’t translate into alpha. It’s all too easy to drown in noise. The real challenge is engineering those terabytes into reliable features, validated models, and real-time indicators that confidently guide decisions under pressure.

Bridging Theory & Production

Elegant PDE solvers, variance-reduced Monte Carlo, or deep-learning architectures on paper become shelfware the moment they hit brittle pipelines. True impact lies in closing the loop: turning academic-grade code into scalable services, wrapping models with APIs and dashboards, and ensuring every new release slides smoothly into your cloud infrastructure.

Democratizing Quant Insights

By surfacing advanced analytics behind intuitive Streamlit apps or Plotly dashboards, you empower traders, risk analysts, and business stakeholders alike to run “what-if” scenarios, explore risk surfaces, and export results—all without delving into the Jupyter notebooks that power them.

Resilience Through Automation

Manual backtests, ad-hoc scripts, and outdated CSV exports lead straight to version-control hell and missed opportunities. Automated CI/CD pipelines, containerised microservices, and orchestrated ETL workflows are the unsung heroes of a modern quant stack, guaranteeing that code, data, and results stay in sync as markets and requirements evolve.

Continuous Calibration & Monitoring

Models degrade. Data drifts. Overnight, your 99%-accurate credit-risk scorecard can become a liability. Instrumenting health checks, anomaly detectors, and automated retraining schedules means you catch breakpoints before they become blowups—keeping production systems robust in the face of shifting regimes.

Toward Next-Gen Financial Tools

From real-time streaming analytics to physics-informed models that respect market microstructure, the frontier of quantitative finance is constantly shifting. Staying ahead demands a mindset of experimentation: rapid prototyping, open-source collaboration, and a relentless focus on reproducibility.

My Work

Here are a few projects I've worked on recently.

Real-World Options Analyser

This interactive Streamlit dashboard allows you to analyse real-world options data for any stock symbol. It calculates Black-Scholes option prices, Greeks, implied volatility, and offers intuitive visualisations to help you understand market sensitivity and time decay.

Risk Analysis for Lending Club

This project builds cost-sensitive machine learning models to predict Lending Club loan default probabilities using only origination features. It explores preprocessing, feature selection, and imbalance handling, comparing logistic regression, XGBoost, and neural networks for robust credit risk assessment.

Trading Strategy Explorer

This project is a complete end-to-end AI-based trading strategy evaluator built using Python and Streamlit. It allows users to analyse stock or crypto tickers and compare an ML-driven trading strategy vs the actual market performance.
